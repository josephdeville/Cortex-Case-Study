Part 2

The Problem & Solution

The Core Problem

Most pipeline problems stem from targeting companies
too early (not ready) or too late (already bought).

  • SDRs prospect based on static ICP criteria
  • No systematic way to identify when a company is ready
  • Win rates suffer, cycles lengthen, forecasts miss

My Hypothesis

Timing is the primary constraint.

The ICP is clear. The product is strong.
What's missing: knowing when a company moves from "interesting" to "ready."

The Solution

Trigger-Based
Signal Engine

Identify companies at the moment they're most likely to need Cortex

Signal Categories

1. Hiring Signals (Strongest)
  • Platform Engineer roles posted
  • "Developer Experience" in titles
  • SRE hiring surge
  • VP/Director of Platform role
2. Technographic Signals
  • Kubernetes adoption
  • Multiple observability tools
  • CI/CD complexity
  • Service mesh (Istio, Linkerd)
3. Organizational Signals
  • Eng headcount growth >20%
  • Series C+ funding
  • New CTO/VP Eng hired
  • Acquisition announced
4. Pain Signals
  • Incident/outage in news
  • Glassdoor: "tech debt"
  • Blog: "microservice challenges"
  • Compliance audit issues

Why Hiring Signals Are Strongest

A company researching "developer portals" might be curious.
A company hiring Platform Engineers is building one.

Job postings = budget allocation, not just interest.
This is the signal with the highest intent.

Scoring Model

ACCOUNT SCORE = Signal Weight × Recency Factor × ICP Fit Signal Weights: Recency Factor: ├─ Platform Engineer posted: +30 ├─ Last 30 days: 1.5x ├─ VP/Dir Platform hired: +25 ├─ 31-60 days: 1.0x ├─ >20% eng growth: +20 ├─ 61-90 days: 0.5x ├─ Kubernetes in stack: +15 └─ >90 days: 0.25x ├─ Recent funding (C+): +15 ├─ Incident in news: +20 ICP Gates (Required): └─ New CTO/VPE: +15 ├─ 1,000+ employees └─ 100+ engineers
